# Gradient-Guided Diffusion-Based Restoration of Extremely Compressed Backgrounds for Video Coding for Machines

## Motivations:
- VCM was initially developed for machine vision, scalable coding frameworks have been developed to support both machine-driven analysis and human viewing as required.
- With decoder: severely degraded BG quality in reconstructed frames makes them unsuitable for viewing;therefore, restoring the degraded BGs by leveraging high-quality ROIs is essential.
- the primary challenge:  restoring the plausibility of the BGs, degraded by aggressive compression.
### Existing solutions:
(i) compression artifact removal: restore entire images degraded by compression artifacts
=>  they may exhibit suboptimal performance in our scenario as they do not effectively account for the semantic disparity between ROIs and BGs, which have different quality levels within the image.

(ii) image inpainting/outpainting: treat the BG as an invalid or missing region, and use valid ROIs as guidance for generating plausible BG content
