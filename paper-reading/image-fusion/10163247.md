# Cross-Modal Transformers for Infrared and Visible Image Fusion

## Abstract:
- Image fusion techniques: generate more informative images by **merging multiple images of different modalities** with complementary information.

### Recent learning-based approaches:
- developed based on convolutional neural networks (CNNs): 
    + stack deep layers to obtain a large receptive field for feature extraction.
    + significant fusion performance improvements,
    + However, important details and contexts of the source images may be lost through a series of convolution layers,

### Propose a cross-modal transformer-based fusion (CMTFusion) algorithm:
- for infrared and visible image fusion 
+ captures global interactions by faithfully extracting complementary information from source images

### The cross-modal transformer-based fusion (CMTFusion) algorithm:
- Step 1: extract the multiscale feature maps of infrared and visible images.
- Step 2: develop cross-modal transformers (CMTs) to retain complementary information in the source images by removing redundancies in both the spatial and channel domains.
    + design a gated bottleneck that integrates cross-domain interaction to consider the characteristics of the source images.
- Step 3: exploiting spatial-channel information in refined feature maps using a fusion block
